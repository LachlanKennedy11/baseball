AHSAA Baseball Rules: An Overview

Slide 1: Player & Team Eligibility

  • Age & Enrollment: To be eligible, a player must be enrolled by the 20th school day of the semester and must not have turned 19 before August 1st of the current school year.
  • Academic Requirements: Students in grades 10-12 must have passed a minimum of six units (including four core courses) with a composite average of 70 in the previous two semesters.
  • Participation Limits: A student is eligible for a maximum of four seasons and eight consecutive semesters after entering the 9th grade.
  • Transfer Rules: A student who transfers must generally attend the new school for one full calendar year to regain athletic eligibility.
  • Non-School Teams: The "Fifty-Percent Rule" may limit how many high school teammates can play on the same non-school team.

Slide 2: Game-Specific Rules

  • Pitch Count is Key: This is a major safety rule.
    • Varsity: 120 pitches max/day
    • JV/Freshmen: 100 pitches max/day
    • Middle School: 85 pitches max/day
  • Mandatory Rest: Required rest periods are based on the number of pitches thrown. For example, a varsity pitcher who throws 76+ pitches needs three calendar days of rest.
  • Game Length: Class 7A teams can play up to 32 games, while Classes 1A-6A can play up to 28 games. No more than two games per day are allowed.
  • Mercy Rule: A game can end after five innings if one team is leading by 10 or more runs.

Slide 3: Key Differences from MLB

  • Re-entry: Starters may re-enter the game once, but must be in their original spot in the batting order.
  • Balks: A balk is an immediate dead ball in high school.
  • Bat Rules: High school bats must meet a specific BBCOR performance standard.
  • Safety & Slides: Rules prohibit "pop-up" or "rolling" slides that make contact with a fielder, as well as hurdling or jumping over a fielder.
  • Double First Base: Starting in the 2027 season, a double first base will be required to prevent collisions.
